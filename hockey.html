Hockey
Hockey is a family of team sports where two opposing teams use hockey sticks to hit a ball or puck into the opponent's goal to score points. Popular variations include field hockey, played on a grass or turf field with 11 players and a ball, and ice hockey, played on a sheet of ice with skates and a puck.

    Historical Context
  • Hockey has roots in ancient forms of the game played in places like Egypt, Ethiopia, and Iran thousands of years ago.
  • The modern game of field hockey emerged in England in the mid-19th century.
  • The International Hockey Federation (FIH) is the global governing body for field hockey.

Five fundamental rules of field hockey:
use only the flat side of the stick to play the ball, do not use your body (except the goalkeeper) to control the ball, goals must be scored from within the designated shooting circle, no contact or obstruction is allowed between players, and it's a foul to hit the ball dangerously or with the rounded side of the stick.
