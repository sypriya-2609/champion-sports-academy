Kabaddi is a traditional Indian contact team sport played by two teams of seven players each, where a raider enters the opponent's court to tag defenders and return safely to their own side while continuously chanting "kabaddi" in a single breath. Originating in ancient India, this sport relies on strength, strategy, and agility, with points scored for successful tags and for successfully tackling the raider. The game, which features a rectangular field divided into two halves, is a significant part of India's physical education curriculum and has gained significant international recognition, particularly after its inclusion in the Asian Games and the launch of the popular Pro Kabaddi League.

    How to Play
  1. Teams & Court: Two teams, each with seven plays, play on a rectangular court divided into two halves by a midline.
  2. The Raid: One player from a team, the "raider," enters the opposing team's half of the court.
  3. Chanting & Breath: The raider must continuously chant "kabaddi, kabaddi" and hold their breath to demonstrate they have not taken a new one.
  4. Scoring Points: Raider: Scores a point for defender they tag and successfully return to their own side without being caught. Defenders: Score a point if they successfully tackle the raider and prevent them from returning to their side.
  5. Getting "Out": A player is "out" if they are tagged by the opposing team's raider or if they are tackled by the defenders.
  6. Winning: The team with the most points at the end of the match wins.
